REGENERATION

TEXT: ROM 6:1-12

06/REF/10/03/2017



Memory Verse: II PETER 3:18

"But grow in spiritual strength and become better acquainted with our Lord saviour Jesus Christ" (LB)


INTRODUCTION

Before something grows again, it has to die, being in a particular position or lacking something which is life. But newness in Christ Jesus is a promise from God through His son, Jesus. II Cor 5:17, Rev 21:5. We are made pure in Him through his blood which he shed on the cross and made new by his resurrection powered by his spirit.



MEANING


    Regeneration is a process to make something grow again (Dictionary meaning). Biblically, it means the process of rising to newness of life in Christ Jesus (Rms 6:4, Gal 6:15, 2, Cor5:17). It means spiritual rebirth, which is a work performed by the holy spirit (Jn 3:6) in the life of a Christian.

    Before the need for something to grow again, there must have been a reduction in value, depreciation or lack of vital things. The certainty that all men are under the influence of sin (I In 1:8) after which redemption came and brought the need to grow again, to become new and be free.

    Using the illustration of the grain of wheat, Jesus explained that it has to die and come back to life in order to bear more. This symbolizes the death of Jesus on the cross, but this seem inadequate enough to bring man into a new birth except by His resurrection (Jn 5:24, I Jn 3:14).

THINGS THAT MAKES US NOT TO GROW

* Sin (works of the flesh) Gal 5:19

* Friendship with the world. JMS 4:4

* Pride. Ish 14:12-14

    Death and resurrection are not enough, to grow, a deep and increased knowledge of the world is needed. It helps us to live a holy life and present us blameless before God always by the renewal of the spirit. (II Peter 1:6a, I Thess 5:23).


THINGS THAT MAKES US GROW

* The word of God (Jn 5:39, II Cor 3:18, II Peter 1:3, 4, Josh 1:8)

* Fasting (Lk 4:1, 2, Dan 9:2, 3, Lk 2:37, Joel 1:13, 14)

* Prayer. (Phil 4:6, 7, Jms 4:2, Eph 6:18)

* Discipleship. (Matt 11:28-30, 4:19-22, Mk 3:13-15, Matt 5:1, 2, 10:1-5)

* Fellowship with other believers. (I Cor 1:9, Eph 2:18, 19, 4:4-6, Heb 10:24, 25)

CONCLUSION

The Holy spirit, the spirit of truth and of liberty is the one that brings about a new life (Jn 3:6, LB). Therefore, it is to the spirit that we have to yield ourselves. We may not know on those He bestows this new life but there are various evidences of this life which differs from man to man (Jn 3:3-8). This new life enables our knowledge Of Christ to be active and effective (II Peter 1:8, GNB). You need to die, leaving the former state to grow in aboundance!
